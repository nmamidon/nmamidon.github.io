List of Unrealistic Expectations

Drop the expectation that I’ll learn JavaScript by summer, and will have a mind mapping app working by then. I won’t, this app will take years. But that’s okay, I want it to be really good, and know I’ll stay interested in it because I’ve wanted this for years anyway.

Instead? I can slowly chip away at JavaScript and the mind mapping app, confident that consistent effort will eventually lead me to these goals, but that it will take a few years. Instead of diving straight into a really complex app I can start by making my own website, which I gotta do anyway. I can make smaller little apps, and really forge a dense (and sparkly) granite foundation to base my work off of. No need to rush, it will take a while.

Another unrelistic expectation is that writing these kinds of things and doing hardcore thinking/reflecting on my life is “time wasted”. It’s not - I need to do it to have clarity and reference. I’m a fallible, crazy human and haven’t memorized my whole ideology here. That said, there is a difference between this kind of writing and writing to procrastinate, wallow, or be self-indulgent. In this, I’m actually trying to focus and learn something from the writing, and have it be useful to read later. Like, I need to read these thoughts, so lucid-past-Nicole can talk down extremely-stressed-future-Nicole. It’s helped already! And also, maybe I can post this stuff somewhere, or make it palatable for others, and hopefully they’ll find it useful as well. (I also have a sneaking suspicion that these kinds of thoughts and reflections are extremely important).

Instead of that unrealistic expectation, I’ll look at writing this stuff like I’ll look at any other thing I’m working on - it’s time well spent, towards doing a specific thing (like how this is specifically, trying out a mindset and set of things to do for next week). And I’ll expect myself to spend time on it each week, and when I’m feeling unsure or unclarified, I’ll know I can read or write to uncover and solidify these aspects of myself.
